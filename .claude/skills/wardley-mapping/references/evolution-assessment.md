# Evolution Assessment Reference

Evolution is measured over **ubiquity × certainty** — NOT over time. Supply/demand competition drives components left to right. The x-axis coordinate is a probability estimate, not a precise measurement.

## Stage Boundaries

| Stage | Maturity Range | Mode |
|---|---|---|
| **Genesis** | 0.0 – 0.20 | Exploration |
| **Custom Built** | 0.20 – 0.40 | Learning |
| **Product** | 0.40 – 0.70 | Refining |
| **Commodity** | 0.70 – 1.00 | Efficiency |

## Evolutionary Characteristics

| Characteristic | Genesis | Custom Built | Product | Commodity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ubiquity** | Rare, one or few examples | Uncommon, few instances | Increasingly common | Widespread, expected everywhere |
| **Certainty** | Poorly understood | Better understood, still learning | Well understood, defined | Fully defined, taken for granted |
| **Market** | Non-existent or grant-based | Emerging bespoke buyers | Competitive, differentiated vendors | Volume operations, price competition |
| **User perception** | Novel/experimental | Competitive advantage | Accepted good practice | Invisible infrastructure |
| **Failure tolerance** | Expected to fail | Moderate tolerance | Low tolerance | Not tolerated — failure is shocking |
| **Value focus** | Discovery | Learning, crafting | Refining, improving | Volume efficiency, cost reduction |
| **Knowledge** | Poorly understood | Tacit, artisan skill | Increasingly documented | Fully codified, textbook |
| **Management** | R&D, experimental | Project-based | Product management | Operations, commodities management |

## Component Type Vocabulary

All types evolve identically; only the terminology differs:

| Type | Genesis | Custom | Product | Commodity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Activity** (what we do) | Genesis | Custom Built | Product | Commodity |
| **Practice** (how we do it) | Novel | Emerging | Good | Best |
| **Data** (how we measure) | Unmodelled | Divergent | Convergent | Modelled |
| **Knowledge** (understanding) | Concept | Hypothesis | Theory | Accepted |

## Three Scoring Questions

1. **Vendor count?**
   - 0–1: Genesis or early Custom Built
   - 2–5 bespoke: Custom Built
   - 5+ differentiated products: Product
   - Many providers, price-based competition: Commodity

2. **Failure response?**
   - Shrug: Genesis
   - Disappointment: Custom Built
   - Frustration/escalation: Product
   - Outrage/headlines/SLA breach: Commodity

3. **Competitor language?**
   - "We invented this": Genesis
   - "Our custom approach is our edge": Custom Built
   - "We use best-of-breed": Product
   - "Of course we have it — everyone does": Commodity

## Common Scoring Traps

**The inertia trap:** Organization treats something as Custom Built because of historical investment, but market has moved to Commodity. Apply `inertia` tag in OWM; flag in commentary.

**The decomposition signal:** If you can't place a component confidently, decompose it. Most "custom" components are commodity sub-components with one genuinely novel part.

**The perception trap:** Score by market reality, not by how the organization currently treats it.
